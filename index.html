Forecasting Electric Energy Pricing

Objective:

Find explanations of the Electric Energy pricing in Colombia for the last 7 years and find a suitable forecasting model.

Rationale:

Get explanations and reasoning behind the energy prices is of utmost importance to make the energy pricing reliable for all the agents in the supply chain of the electric power system.

Data science tools required:

  • Deep Learning
  • Long Short-Term Memory Networks
  • Multilinear regression
  • Power Bi

Reports (Spanish):
  • Forecasting - Energy pricing (PDF)

Acknowledge:

This project is part of the college class: Ciencia de Datos Energéticos from the Universidad Nacional de Colombia

Consultance in Human Resources Department

Objective:

Pinpoint reasons for the employees to leave the organization and define strategies encourage them to continue in the company.

Rationale:

The hiring process represents a high cost for any company. The time required to gather talented individuals that draw value to the organization. Hence, motivating the employee to stay in the company is a high priority task for the Human Resources Department in every company.

Data science tools required:

  • Artificial Neural Networks
  • Logistic Regression Classifier
  • Random Forest Classiffier

About

I am an objective-driven individual with a structured mindset and a strong desire to learn and specialize in topics that I am passionate about. This passion led me to pursue a Master's degree even before completing my Bachelor's degree, and I am currently engaged in thesis research.

To date, I have developed proficiency in Python and its data science libraries, including scikit, statsmodels, numpy, pandas, and matplotlib. I possess knowledge in data cleansing, regression (linear, multilinear, and logistic), and I have also studied time-series analysis using methods such as multi-input LSTM neural networks and ARIMA. Additionally, I have gained practical experience by developing Power BI dashboards for non-technical stakeholders, and I have acquired a certification in this field from Udemy.

My work has primarily revolved around academic projects related to visualizing, explaining, and forecasting Electric Energy datasets in order to draw meaningful conclusions. Furthermore, I have gained valuable experience working with Black Box Data from PowerFlex 7000 drives, enabling me to identify behavioral patterns and pinpoint potential causes of faults.

During my tenure at Rockwell Automation, I have consistently demonstrated excellence in completing assigned projects withing an agile framework and and actively engaged in teamwork. The success of these projects is evidenced by their active status, as well as their global impact, reaching over 1,000 registered users and receiving recognition in the Global Employee Meetings for LCS.
